MULTIPLE SCLEROSIS

The goal of this MS forum is to find out more about this disease directly from people suffering from it or relatives who take care of them. We'll welcome and appreciate anyone who will voluntarily share their experiences through the course of this disease with the members of the forum. MS is a complex disease and therefore, as a mediator, I will suggest a few topics related to MS  ( e.g. nutrition ) and you will chose on which you want to reply ( could be one or more ).
I ask all interested in posting to take this delicate topic with respect and seriousness. All improper text containing insults, profanities and alike will be discarded.

Submit

    Suggested  discussion:
    - Unpleasant sensations in the body-description  (  e.g. location, intensity on scale 0-10, frequency and duration, description of it in your own words, any peculiarities, any additional observation or comments ).
    - Nutrition and course of MS ( e.g.what kind of diet, aggravation or improvement of MS, duration of improvement e.t.c. ) 
    - Therapy ( e.g. what kind of therapy, improvement or aggravation e.t.c. )
    - Level of vitamin D3 & B12 in blood _                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          

    Please describe in your own words if you experience any unpleasant sensations in your body in as much detail as you can.The purpose of this is to unveil the nature of these sensations which are usually put under the same roof as pain, although they are distinguished entities. Many times these sensations as for example "cold burning" as some would call them , are hard to describe to those who are not MS patients and even to their doctors due to lack of proper words in our vocabulary.Only those hit by MS can understand what their fellow sufferers go through on daily basis. Therefore, by telling us your story and what you feel, you will contribute to better understanding of this disease, perhaps even help in finding a better , more appropriate name for these dreadful and annoying sensations . At the same time other MS patients who will read your testimonials will know that they are not alone.
